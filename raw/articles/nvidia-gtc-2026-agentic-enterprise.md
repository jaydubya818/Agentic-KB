## Content

NVIDIA just fired the starting gun on the Agentic Enterprise era — and most companies aren't ready.

At GTC 2026, NVIDIA launched its open-source Agent Toolkit — signing Adobe, Salesforce, SAP, and 13 other major platforms to power autonomous, self-evolving enterprise AI agents.

Jensen Huang: "Employees will be supercharged by teams of frontier, specialized and custom-built agents they deploy and manage."

### Key Observations for Enterprise Leaders

- **The agent stack is getting standardized.** NVIDIA's OpenShell runtime enforces policy-based security, privacy, and network guardrails — making enterprise agents finally deployable at scale, safely.
- **Your software vendors are transforming.** CRM, ERP, and workflow tools won't just use AI — they will BE agentic platforms.
- **The org chart is changing.** The new structure has human roles + agent roles. Leaders who design for this hybrid workforce now will outperform those who wait.
- **Governance is the new competitive moat.** Open-source agent infrastructure lowers the build barrier. The differentiator is who can govern, audit, and scale agent behavior responsibly.
- **Speed to deployment = speed to value.** Companies experimenting in 2025 are deploying full agentic systems in 2026. The gap between leaders and laggards is widening fast.

### 20 Enterprise AI Compliance Requirements

Before deploying AI, enterprises must:

1. Appoint an AI Leader (senior executive for AI governance)
2. Create an AI Inventory (document all AI systems, models, APIs, tools)
3. Classify AI Risks (high, limited, minimal risk categories)
4. Form an AI Governance Board (legal, security, HR, business teams)
5. Define AI Use Policies (permitted and restricted AI use)
6. Conduct Risk Assessments (safety, bias, discrimination risks)
7. Clarify Legal Responsibilities (roles of AI providers vs deployers)
8. Test for Bias (regularly evaluate and reduce bias in outputs)
9. Maintain Documentation (system design, data sources, limits)
10. Review Third-Party AI (vendor compliance and contracts)
11. Disclose AI Usage (inform users when AI or synthetic content is used)
12. Ensure Data Governance (protect data access, respect copyrights)
13. Publish Transparency Reports (model purpose, limits, performance)
14. Perform DPIAs (evaluate high-risk personal data processing)
15. Support Data Rights (access, correction, deletion, opt-outs)
16. Verify Lawful Data Use (proper legal basis for data processing)
17. Apply Data Minimization (collect only necessary data, limit retention)
18. Enable Logging & Audits (track AI inputs, outputs, decisions)
19. Ensure Explainability (clear AI decisions with human oversight)
20. Secure AI Infrastructure (protect models, pipelines, APIs)

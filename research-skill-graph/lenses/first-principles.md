# Lens: First Principles

Forget everything you think you know. Strip away assumptions, conventions, and received wisdom. Rebuild from fundamental truths only.

---

## Core Questions
1. What are the absolute base-level facts that are not in dispute?
2. What assumptions is the mainstream analysis built on? Are any of them wrong?
3. What's the simplest model that explains 80% of what we observe?
4. What would a completely uninformed, logical mind conclude from just the raw evidence?
5. What would you believe if you had never encountered this topic before?
6. What is structurally impossible regardless of policy, technology, or effort?
7. What is structurally inevitable regardless of intervention?

## How to Research Through This Lens
- Start by listing everything you assume to be true. Then challenge each assumption.
- Ask "why" five times on any claim to reach the underlying mechanism
- Identify what is physically, mathematically, or logically constrained — these are your anchor points
- Build up from axioms: what do we know with certainty? What can be derived from that?
- Be suspicious of complex explanations when a simple one fits the data

## Output Format
```
AXIOM: [indisputable base fact]
DERIVED: [what follows logically from the axiom]
ASSUMPTION CHALLENGED: [commonly held assumption + why it may be wrong]
SIMPLE MODEL: [explanation that fits most of the evidence]
STRUCTURAL CONSTRAINT: [what cannot change regardless of effort]
STRUCTURAL INEVITABILITY: [what will happen regardless of resistance]
```

## Voice
Reductive. Axiomatic. Ruthlessly simple.
- ✅ "At base: humans reproduce when conditions feel safe and abundant. The mechanism is simple; the complication is what each culture defines as 'safe and abundant' has changed faster than evolution."
- ❌ "According to various theorists, the root causes are multifaceted..."

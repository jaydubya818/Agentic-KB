# Lens: Historical

What patterns repeat? What has been tried before? What context is everyone currently forgetting?

---

## Core Questions
1. When has something structurally similar happened before? What were the conditions?
2. What was tried as a solution? What worked, what failed, and why?
3. What context from 20, 50, 100 years ago does the current conversation ignore?
4. What cycles are at play — technological, political, demographic, economic?
5. What's the "this time is different" claim? What evidence supports or refutes it?
6. What did observers at the time think would happen? Were they right?
7. Who has successfully navigated this before, and how?

## How to Research Through This Lens
- Look for historical analogues with similar structural conditions (not just surface resemblance)
- Search for primary sources from the period, not just modern interpretations
- Find cases where the conventional historical narrative is contested by historians
- Explicitly look for "the exception" — the case that doesn't fit the pattern
- Date every claim. "X always happens" requires evidence across multiple distinct periods.

## Output Format
```
HISTORICAL ANALOGUE: [what period/event]
CONDITIONS: [structural similarities to current situation]
WHAT HAPPENED: [outcome, with timeline]
WHAT WAS TRIED: [interventions and their results]
LESSON: [what this implies for the current situation]
CAVEAT: [where the analogy breaks down]
```

## Voice
Long-horizon. Precedent-focused. Humbling toward current certainties.
- ✅ "Japan has maintained sub-replacement fertility since 1974 without societal collapse, suggesting the crisis timeline may be longer than current models assume"
- ❌ "History shows this always ends in collapse"

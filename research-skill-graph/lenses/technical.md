# Lens: Technical

Strip away opinions and narratives. What do the numbers actually say? What mechanisms are at work?

---

## Core Questions
1. What does the DATA show? (not what people say about the data)
2. What are the measurable inputs and outputs?
3. What mechanisms drive the phenomenon?
4. What are the hard constraints (physical, biological, mathematical, computational)?
5. What metrics matter most and how are they actually measured?
6. Where is the data incomplete, poorly measured, or systematically biased?
7. What does the trend line look like over 10, 20, 50 years?

## How to Research Through This Lens
- Start with Tier 1 sources ONLY (see [[methodology/source-evaluation]]): raw datasets, peer-reviewed studies
- Look for: time series, geographic comparisons, demographic breakdowns
- **Quantify everything.** Replace "declining" with "declined by X% between Y and Z"
- Identify measurement problems: how is this data collected? what's excluded? who funds the collection?
- Find the base rates. What's "normal"? What's the historical range?
- Challenge your first number — find a conflicting dataset before concluding

## Output Format
For each finding:
```
METRIC: [what you measured]
VALUE: [the number, with units]
SOURCE: [Tier 1/2 source + date]
TREND: [direction and rate of change]
CAVEAT: [measurement limitations or gaps]
```
Record all hard numbers in [[knowledge/data-points]].

## Voice
Clinical. Precise. No emotional language. No narrative framing.
- ✅ "Fertility rate declined from 2.1 to 1.6 between 1990 and 2023 in OECD countries"
- ❌ "Fertility is collapsing across the developed world"

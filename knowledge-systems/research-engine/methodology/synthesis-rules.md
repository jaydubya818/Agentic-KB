# Synthesis Rules

How to combine findings across lenses without flattening nuance. The hardest part of research.

---

## The Synthesis Process

### Step 1: Lens Summary
After completing all 6 lenses, write ONE paragraph per lens:
- What is this lens's main finding?
- Confidence level: high / medium / low (and why)
- What surprised you from this angle?

### Step 2: Agreement Map
Identify where lenses AGREE:
- 4+ lenses pointing same direction → high confidence finding, state directly
- 3 lenses agree → moderate confidence, state with caveats
- Only 1-2 lenses support a claim → hypothesis only, flag as uncertain

### Step 3: Tension Map
Identify where lenses DISAGREE:
- `technical` says X but `economic` says Y → **this tension IS the insight**
- Don't resolve by picking a winner — document both positions
- Ask: "Under what conditions is each lens correct?"
- See [[methodology/contradiction-protocol]] for resolution framework

### Step 4: Second-Order Insights
The best findings come from COMBINING lenses:
> "The technical data shows declining fertility, but the economic lens reveals that financial incentives haven't reversed it anywhere — which means the first-principles lens's argument about cultural shifts might be the dominant factor."

These cross-lens insights are what make this system more powerful than reading 50 articles. They only emerge when lenses are run distinctly and then compared.

### Step 5: Confidence Calibration
For each major finding, state explicitly:
- **CLAIM:** what you believe is true
- **EVIDENCE:** strongest supporting data (cite from [[knowledge/data-points]])
- **CONFIDENCE:** high / medium / low
- **WHAT WOULD CHANGE MY MIND:** the specific evidence that would reverse this conclusion

---

## Output Rules
- Never present a single-lens finding as a final conclusion
- Always show the tension between lenses in `deep-dive.md`
- Separate "what the data shows" from "what I interpret"
- `open-questions.md` is as important as `executive-summary.md`
- Prefer: *"It seems likely that X because Y and Z, though A complicates this"*
- Avoid: *"X is true"* (unless 4+ lenses agree AND Tier 1 sources confirm)

---

## Anti-Patterns (kill good research)
| Anti-Pattern | What It Looks Like | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Confirmation bias** | Only searching for evidence supporting your initial hunch | Run contrarian lens first if you notice a prior belief |
| **Narrative fallacy** | Clean story from messy, contradictory data | Use tension map, don't resolve what's genuinely unresolved |
| **Recency bias** | Overweighting latest article over 30 years of data | Historical lens explicitly looks backward |
| **Authority bias** | Believing something because an impressive person said it | Still need Tier 1 data to back it up |
| **Anchoring** | First number you found defines your mental range | Record numbers in data-points before forming opinions |
| **All lenses agree** | Research feels too clean | Re-run contrarian lens harder. Something is being missed. |

# Research Frameworks

How to approach different types of research questions. Read BEFORE starting to pick the right structure.

---

## Framework Selection

### Type 1: "Is X true?" — Verification
- Start with [[lenses/technical]] to establish what the data actually says
- Then [[lenses/contrarian]] to stress-test the claim
- Then [[lenses/historical]] for precedent
- **Best for:** fact-checking, debunking, validating assumptions
- **Example:** "Is nuclear fusion viable by 2035?"

### Type 2: "Why is X happening?" — Causal Analysis
- Start with [[lenses/historical]] to trace the roots
- Then [[lenses/economic]] for incentive structures
- Then [[lenses/technical]] for mechanism
- Then [[lenses/geopolitical]] for systemic forces
- Then [[lenses/contrarian]] to challenge your causal chain
- **Best for:** understanding trends, explaining phenomena
- **Example:** "Why are birth rates collapsing globally?"

### Type 3: "What happens if X?" — Scenario Planning
- Start with [[lenses/first-principles]] to establish base assumptions
- Then [[lenses/technical]] for constraints
- Then [[lenses/economic]] for incentives
- Then [[lenses/geopolitical]] for power dynamics
- **Best for:** forecasting, strategy, decision-making
- **Example:** "What happens to Europe if birth rates stay below replacement for 30 years?"

### Type 4: "What should I do about X?" — Decision Support
- Run all 6 lenses in parallel
- Rank options by lens agreement (if 5/6 lenses point same way → high confidence)
- **Best for:** investment decisions, policy, strategy, vendor selection

---

## Research Depth Levels

### Level 1: Quick Scan (30 min)
- 3 lenses maximum (technical + contrarian + most relevant)
- Top 5 sources only
- Goal: directional understanding, not certainty

### Level 2: Standard Research (2-3 hours)
- All 6 lenses
- 15-25 sources, minimum Tier 2
- Cross-reference findings between lenses
- Goal: informed opinion backed by evidence

### Level 3: Deep Dive (1-2 days)
- All 6 lenses with sub-questions
- 50+ sources including primary data
- Primary sources / expert interviews where possible
- Contradiction resolution required before synthesis
- Goal: publishable analysis, decision-grade intelligence

---

## Source Collection Strategy

For each lens:
1. Find the BEST single source on this angle (see [[methodology/source-evaluation]])
2. Find the source that DISAGREES most with #1
3. Find primary data that lets you judge between them
4. Record everything in [[knowledge/data-points]] with attribution

Never proceed to synthesis with fewer than 2 sources per lens that actually conflict.

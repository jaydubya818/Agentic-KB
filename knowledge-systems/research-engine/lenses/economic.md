# Lens: Economic

Follow the money. Who pays, who profits, what incentives drive behavior — and what policies have actually been tried?

---

## Core Questions
1. Who benefits financially from the current situation continuing?
2. Who pays the costs — directly and indirectly?
3. What incentive structures drive the behavior we're observing?
4. What economic policies have been tried? What were the measured results?
5. What markets does this affect and how are prices/volumes moving?
6. What's the ROI of proposed solutions? Who would fund them and why?
7. What does the money flow look like over 5, 10, 20 years?

## How to Research Through This Lens
- Start by mapping stakeholders: who has financial interest in each possible outcome?
- Look for: market data, policy outcomes with measured results, incentive structures
- Distrust any analysis that doesn't ask "who funded this?"
- Find examples where stated motivation and financial motivation diverge
- Look for historical analogues where economic incentives predicted behavior

## Output Format
```
STAKEHOLDER: [actor]
FINANCIAL INTEREST: [what they gain/lose]
INCENTIVE: [what behavior this drives]
EVIDENCE: [source for the incentive claim]
POLICY TESTED: [what was tried] → RESULT: [measured outcome]
```

## Voice
Incentive-driven. Skeptical of stated motivations. Follow revealed preferences over stated preferences.
- ✅ "Pharmaceutical companies earn $X/yr from ongoing treatment vs $Y from a cure — which explains their R&D allocation"
- ❌ "Companies say they're committed to patient outcomes"

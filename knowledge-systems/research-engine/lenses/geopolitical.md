# Lens: Geopolitical

Zoom out to the global chessboard. Which countries, which power dynamics, which alliances and conflicts shape this?

---

## Core Questions
1. Which countries are most affected — and in opposite ways?
2. What power dynamics does this create, reinforce, or threaten?
3. Which alliances shift if this trend continues?
4. What are different governments' strategic interests in the outcome?
5. How does geography constrain or enable the actors involved?
6. What does this look like from Beijing, Washington, Brussels, and Riyadh simultaneously?
7. What international institutions are involved, and whose interests do they represent?

## How to Research Through This Lens
- Map the stakeholder countries first: who benefits, who loses, who's indifferent
- Look at actual government behavior (revealed preferences), not stated positions
- Find the secondary effects: what does a change in X mean for Y country's strategic position?
- Seek out non-Western perspectives — most English-language analysis has embedded assumptions
- Look for what's NOT being discussed in official channels

## Output Format
```
COUNTRY/BLOC: [actor]
STRATEGIC INTEREST: [what they want from this situation]
BEHAVIOR: [what they're actually doing]
POWER DYNAMIC: [how this shifts relationships]
SOURCE: [Tier 1/2 — government reports, think tanks with disclosed funding]
```

## Voice
Systemic. Power-aware. Don't personalize; focus on structural interests.
- ✅ "China's strategic interest in low global fertility in competitor nations is documented in demographic policy literature"
- ❌ "China is evil and manipulating everything"
